Bio Sketch

I am an astrophysicist, specialising in the physics of solar flares, originally from Scotland and now based in Washington D.C. at NASA GSFC. Most of my work involves simulating solar flares, but I also work with observations from several NASA assets and ground based observatories to both validate and critique those simulations. My Research

Solar flares are dramatic energy release events in the Sun's atmosphere, which together with coronal mass ejections, comprise a fundamental component of space weather. I research the flow of mass, energy, and radiation through the solar atmosphere during flares and energetic events, with a particular focus on the complex plasma environment of the solar chromosphere. This is achieved via state-of-the-art computer simulations of the response of the Sun's atmosphere to flare energy injection, and the formation of radiation during these events. Observations from NASA spacecraft and ground based observatories can be used to determine how well these models capture the physical processes at play in flares. If our models fail to stand up to the scrutiny of those observations we know that we are missing ingredients (physics) which then must investigate.
